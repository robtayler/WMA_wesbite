What is a kata?

Kata (形, or more traditionally, 型) (literally: "form") is a Japanese word describing detailed patterns of movements practiced either solo or in pairs. Each is a complete fighting system, with the movements and postures of the kata being a living reference guide to the correct form and structure of the techniques used within that system. Karate Kata are executed as a specified series of a variety of moves, with stepping and turning, while attempting to maintain perfect form. The practitioner is counseled to visualise the enemy attacks and their responses. Karateka "read" a kata in order to explain the imagined events. The kata is not intended as a literal depiction of a mock fight, but as a display of transition and flow from one posture and movement to another, teaching the student proper form and position, and encouraging them to visualise different scenarios for the use of each motion and technique. There are various forms of kata, each with many minor variations.

How to improve your side kick in 3 easy steps

Posted 45 minutes ago

The side kick is a powerful kick if used correctly, but if not it can lack power and flick like a poorly formatted roundhouse. Step one: have a good chamber position. Step two: turn the base foot to point 180 degree from the direction of the attacker, and to rotate the hips for full extension. Step three. foot forming, point toes up to extend the heel and direct the power towards your target.

Rules and Etiquette

  1. No rings, watches or chains to be worn whilst training.
  2. On entering and leaving the dojo,students must bow.
  3. Instructors have the right to ask disruptive students to leave the club.
  4. No eating, drinking or smoking in the dojo.
  5. All students must be in a suitable training clothes if they do not have a Gi.
  6. Gi's must be clean and tidy at all times.
  7. No shoes or socks to be worn whilst training.
  8. finger and toenails must be kept clean and short.
  9. Students must only use the techniques that they have been shown in defense not aggression.
  10. Respect must be given to all students whatever grade.
  11. Aggression must always be controlled in the dojo.
  12. We advise all students to take out membership and insurance as soon as possible

VISITORS ARE ALWAYS WELCOME, SUBJECT TO THE ABOVE RULES.
